Call for Papers

TAAI 2021 consists of both the international track and domestic track. Papers on academic researches and industrial applications of artificial intelligence and related areas are welcome. TAAI 2021 is open to international participants. We also welcome experienced scholars to organize special sessions to detail specific topics. Accepted papers will be submitted for inclusion into the IEEE Digital Library (IEL) and Scopus Database. Selected papers from the international track will be invited to submit an extended version for a special issue in the Journal of Information Science and Engineering (JSIE, indexed by SCIE) after the conference.


Topics of Interest

The conference will cover a wide range of AI research topics and applications, including but not limited to:

  • Autonomous Agents and Distributed AI
  • Computer Games
  • Computer Vision / Pattern Recognition
  • Data Mining and Knowledge Discovery
  • Federated Learning
  • Fuzzy Systems and Fuzzy Neural Networks
  • Genetic and Evolutionary Computation
  • Knowledge Representation and Automatic Reasoning
  • Knowledge-Based Systems
  • Machine Learning
  • Natural Language Processing
  • Problem Solving and Search
  • Recommender Systems
  • Reinforcement Learning
  • Speech Recognition and Synthesis
  • Web Intelligence and Social Networks


Submission

International track: Papers for the international track have to be written and presented in English. Manuscripts should be submitted in PDF format and formatted using the IEEE 2-column format, including the bibliography within 6 pages. Submissions to TAAI 2021 are single-blind, i.e. the names affiliations of the authors should be listed in the submitted version.

Important information:

  • IEEE Manuscript Templates： Download
  • At least one author per accepted paper must register and attend the conference to present for the accepted submission to be included in the conference proceedings.
    • 　
  • Accepted papers will be submitted for inclusion into IEEE Xplore.

Instructions regarding camera ready and registration:

  • Authors of accepted papers should submit the camera-ready copy before Oct. 08, 2021. Each International Track paper (including three Special Sessions) should provide the copyright form as the following steps:
    1. Authors should log in to the IEEE eCF site to submit a copyright form. The first time to log in to the IEEE eCF site, please use "Sign up" directly. Enter your email and initial password to log in to the system.
    2. After filling out the IEEE copyright form on the IEEE eCF site, it will generate the copyright form.
    3. The authors need to download and upload it with the camera-ready copy to the CMT submission system simultaneously. The CMT system allows multiple files upload.
    • 　
  • Papers that do not have a full-registered author by the registration deadline (Oct. 08, 2021) will be removed from the proceedings. In the case of multiple acceptances, each extra paper requires at least one extra Full Registration.
  • If you have travel restrictions due to COVID-19, you may still publish your paper at TAAI 2021 conference with discounted fee.
